24 Hour Guide

Seattle offers so much to see and do that you could easily fill a week with activities, but if you only have 24 hours, here is a jam-packed day of highlights that will take you from historic landmarks to scenic water views and an evening show.

9:00AM - Space Needle* - Start your day at Seattle’s most iconic structure, the Space Needle. Originally built in 1962, the deck of the Space Needle stands 520 feet in the air and offers 360 degree views of Seattle, Mount Rainer, and the Puget Sound.

10:30PM - Chihuly Garden and Glass* - Walk through scenic gardens and indoor galleries featuring the colorful glass artwork of Dale Chihuly.

12:00PM - Seattle Center Armory Food & Event Hall – Watch a performance on the Center Stage and enjoy lunch at one of the Armory restaurants.

1:30PM - Pacific Science Center* - Explore the science center where you can watch a music laser show, experience a planetarium presentation, or view one of the center’s ongoing exhibits including a butterfly house and innovation lab.

3:00PM – Museum of Pop Culture* - View film, fashion, music, and gaming through the lens of pop culture in this unique museum.

4:30PM - Catch the monorail from Seattle Center to Westlake Center. From Westlake Center it’s a short walk to your evening destinations.

5:00PM – Explore Pike Market Place, a nine-acre public market that includes 500 small businesses and eateries. Shop, be entertained, and find some delicious food for dinner.

6:30PM – From Pike Market Place, a half mile walk will take you along the Alaskan Way waterfront where you can see both local ferry boats and huge cruise ships.

7:00PM – Take in the city and the Puget Sound from high above as you ride the 175 foot tall Seattle Great Wheel. After dark the wheel lights up with over 500,000 LED lights.

8:00PM – Wrap up your day with music, dancing, or a show at Seattle’s Crocodile Venues, a triple entertainment experience with daily performances.

* Admission included with a City Pass

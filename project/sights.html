Sights

Seattle has something for everyone... from scenic landscapes and water views to iconic landmarks and museums to music, sports, and tech companies, the city is full of places to visit and things to see. The city is easily walkable, or visitors can catch the Monorail that travels between Westlake Center and Seattle Center to access many of Seattle’s most popular attractions.

Explore the following or look for more Things to Do at Visit Seattle.

Within walking distance of the Seattle Center Monorail station:

Space Needle

Visit this iconic landmark to experience a revolving glass floor and breathtaking views of Seattle and the surrounding area.

Museum of Pop Culture

View film, fashion, science fiction, music, and gaming through the lens of pop culture in this unique museum.

Gates Foundation Discovery Center

Explore exhibits that showcase how the Gates Foundation is striving to make the world a better place.

Seattle Opera

Attend a spectacular performance or learn more about opera through classes and community discussion events.

Olympic Sculpture Park

Take a free tour of this outdoor landscape that reflects the native ecosystems of the Pacific Northwest.

Seattle Center Armory Food & Event Hall

Watch a performance on the Center Stage or enjoy a meal at one of the Armory restaurants.

Chihuly Garden and Glass

Walk through scenic gardens and indoor galleries featuring the colorful glass artwork of Dale Chihuly.

Pacific Science Center

Explore this expansive venue that includes an IMAX theater, planetarium, laser dome, and many science exhibits.

Within walking distance of the Westlake Center Monorail station:

Seattle Great Wheel

Take a ride on this 175 foot Ferris wheel that overlooks the Seattle waterfront and offers scenic views of the city.

Seattle Aquarium

Learn about animals, ecosystems, and conservation efforts in this aquatic center that spans three buildings.

Seattle Art Museum

Browse exhibits featuring art from around the world or attend one of the museum’s many events or workshops.

Pike Place Market

Traverse nine-acres and 500 small businesses including crafters, shops, restaurants, farmers, and buskers.

Museum of Illusions

Immerse yourself in a world of illusions and images that will challenge your perception and perspective.
